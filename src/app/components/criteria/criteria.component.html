ICA Statement on Cooperative Identity

Fundamental Basis for Cooperative Cultural Heritage

Cooperative Cultural Heritage Assessment

Explore the importance of preserving cultural heritage through cooperative initiatives. This section highlights how tradition and modern values align through collaboration.

The Statement declares that: A cooperative is an autonomous association of people who voluntarily unite to meet their common economic, social, and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly owned and democratically controlled enterprise. This definition highlights that cooperatives are not merely economic organizations but living cultural systems. They embody communities’ ability to shape their own future through solidarity, participation, and shared responsibility. Cooperatives safeguard traditions of mutual aid and respond to evolving cultural needs and aspirations, making them an integral part of humanity’s intangible cultural heritage while simultaneously functioning as people-driven enterprises. These universal principles of cooperation resonate with the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR) and the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR), both legally binding human rights instruments that enshrine fundamental rights to participation, equality, cultural life, and self-determination. Cooperatives give practical expression to these rights by creating institutions through which people collectively govern their economic and social lives while preserving their cultural distinctiveness. Cooperators are guided by the ethical values of honesty, openness, social responsibility, and care for others, which represent a cultural legacy of cooperation, shaping communities and inspiring the safeguarding of cooperative heritage. The Cooperative Principles translate these values into practices that carry forward the cooperative tradition and, in the context of Cooperative Cultural Heritage, express the following:

The Seven Cooperative Principles

Guiding values that shape inclusion, democracy, fairness, and community well-being.

1

Voluntary and Open Association

Emphasizes inclusion and a sense of belonging.

2

Democratic Member Control

Ensures equality in governance.

3

Member Economic Participation

Ensures fairness in the distribution of benefits.

4

Autonomy and Independence

Preserves the integrity of collective self-determination.

5

Education, Training, and Information

Supports the intergenerational transmission of cooperative culture.

6

Cooperation among Cooperatives

Strengthens solidarity within and between communities.

7

Concern for the Community

Ensures cooperative action promotes cultural vitality, social well-being, and environmental sustainability.

These values and principles inspire the ICA CCH Standards, which identify and safeguard the heritage of cooperation as an expression of community identity, cultural continuity, and democratic practice.
