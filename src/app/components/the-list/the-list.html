Expression of Cooperative
Cultural Heritage

An expression of Cooperative Cultural Heritage is a distinctive practice, place, tradition,
institution, or system that embodies the values, identity, and legacy of cooperation.

Tangible:

Buildings, settlements, archives, landscapes, ...

Intangible:

Practices, traditions, knowledge, skills, expressions, ...

Each heritage element is recognized because:

1.

Embodies cooperative principles (democracy, fairness, solidarity, mutual support).

2.

Holds cultural, social, or historical significance for a community or region.

3.

Conveys cooperative identity across generations.

4.

Demonstrates continuity and innovation, connecting past traditions with present and future needs.

How will the cultural
expressions be selected?

The selection will follow clear and transparent standards to reflect the
diversity and richness of the global cooperative tradition.

Identification Criteria

A cultural expression should meet most or all of the following
fundamental criteria

Rooted in cooperative principles

GEmbodies core cooperative values: democracy, equality, equity, solidarity, and self-help. Demonstrates shared ownership or governance.

Cultural and social significance

Holds importance for a community’s identity, memory, or continuity. Contributes to social cohesion, mutual support, or cultural expression.

Historical or heritage value

Represents milestones in cooperative development. Preserves stories, traditions, or knowledge tied to cooperative evolution.

Transmission across generations

Actively practiced, preserved, or interpreted by communities. Provides educational value, inspiring younger generations.

Continuity and innovation

Demonstrates adaptation of cooperative practices to changing contexts. Balances preservation with creativity and renewal.

Community ownership and participation

Named and governed with active member involvement. Reflects collective consent and participation in its recognition.
